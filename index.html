Ordering Policy
KCHC Anticoagulation Policy · Updated: May 2025
Primary Source: Justin Wilson, PharmD Presentation
  • Ordered as a one-time order, repeated daily
  • New starts: baseline INR within 24 hours must be in EPIC
  • All subsequent orders: INR within 24 hours prior to order
  • Standard initiation: 5 mg daily
  • Sensitive patients (age >75, ESRD, CHF, hepatic disease, malnutrition, malignancy): consider 1–2.5 mg daily
INR Monitoring — What the KCHC Protocol Says
New Start — Initiation Phase
  • Day 1: No INR required — give selected starting dose
  • Day 2 onward: INR must be drawn and resulted in EPIC within 24 hours before every warfarin order — this is KCHC policy, not optional
  • Warfarin is a one-time daily order — it must be re-ordered each day, and the 24h INR rule applies to every re-order
  • Continue daily INR until 2 consecutive therapeutic INR values are achieved
Maintenance Phase
  • KCHC policy: INR required within 24 hours prior to each warfarin order — applies throughout the hospital stay
  • Stable outpatient monitoring intervals (weekly → biweekly → monthly) are not specified in this presentation
  • For stable patient intervals: refer to Shareplus → Pharmacy → Staff → Clinical → Anticoagulants
Argatroban → Warfarin Transition
  • Check INR 4 hours after stopping argatroban infusion
  • INR 2–3: continue warfarin monotherapy
  • INR <2: restart argatroban · repeat INR check following day
  • See HIT / Argatroban tab for full transition protocol

When to Obtain Anti-Xa Level — Indications
KCHC Anticoagulation Policy · May 2025
Justin Wilson, PharmD Presentation · March 2026

Anti-Xa monitoring is recommended in the following patient populations:

  • BMI ≥40 — therapeutic or prophylactic dosing
  • Weight >150 kg — standard weight-based dosing may be unreliable
  • Pregnancy — pharmacokinetics altered; Anti-Xa required for all therapeutic doses
  • CrCl <30 mL/min — accumulation risk; monitor closely
  • Underweight — Female <45 kg or Male <57 kg
Draw Timing
  • BID regimens: draw 4 hours after the 3rd dose (steady state)
  • QD regimens: draw 4 hours after the dose
  • Trough (optional): draw before next dose — goal <0.5 units/mL
Target Ranges
  • BID therapeutic: 0.6–1.0 units/mL
  • QD therapeutic: 1.0–2.0 units/mL (KCHC Policy May 2025)
  • Prophylactic (adult): 0.1–0.3 units/mL
  • Pediatric prophylactic: 0.1–0.3 units/mL

Hold Before Procedure
  • Low bleed risk: hold 24 hours before
  • Moderate–high bleed risk: hold 48 hours before
Restart After Procedure
  • After adequate hemostasis: 24–72 hours post-surgery
Reversal — Kcentra (4-PCC)
  • Bleed reversal: 2,000 units × 1 (preferred) or 25–50 units/kg (max 5,000)

  • Pre-emergent procedure: 2,000 units × 1

Cockcroft-Gault · Body Weight · Vancomycin Rules
KCHC Antimicrobial Stewardship · P&T Approved: Oct 2020
Cockcroft-Gault CrCl Formula
CrCl = [(140 − age) × weight] ÷ (72 × SCr) × gender factor

Gender factor: Male = 1.0 · Female = 0.85
Weight: Use IBW. If ABW < IBW, use ABW. If ABW > 130% IBW, use AdjBW.
AdjBW = IBW + 0.4 × (ABW − IBW)

Note: If elderly + low ABW + SCr <0.8, consider rounding SCr to 0.8.
IBW Formula
Male IBW = 50 kg + 2.3 kg per inch over 5 feet
Female IBW = 45.5 kg + 2.3 kg per inch over 5 feet
Vancomycin Hold Rule
If trough >25 mcg/mL → HOLD dose
Recheck level every 6–12 hours
Re-dose only when level <20 mcg/mL
Max single dose: 2,000 mg (ABW-based)
IHD Dosing Rule
